Tonight's Screening

A presenter guide for running slides and trailers

The Big Picture

You will use two apps tonight — and only two apps. Close everything else on the computer before you begin. No extra browser tabs, no email, no Slack. A clean screen means fewer surprises.

  1. Google Slides (in Chrome) — for the presentation deck
  2. VLC — for the opening and closing trailers

Slides run in your browser. VLC runs in its own window. You will switch between them with Cmd + Tab.

Well Before the Show

Download VLC in advance VLC is free and available at videolan.org. Download and install it on the presenting computer before the event — not the night of. Venue Wi-Fi can be slow or unreliable, and you do not want to depend on it.
Clear the desktop Before you set anything up, close every open app, tab, and window on the computer. You want only two things running: Chrome with the Google Slides tab, and VLC. Notifications, too — turn on Do Not Disturb (Control + Click the clock in the menu bar, or open System Settings → Focus → Do Not Disturb) so nothing pops up mid-presentation.

15 Minutes Before

Do these steps at least 15 minutes before the audience arrives.

Set Up VLC

  1. Open VLC Find VLC in your Applications folder (or Spotlight: Cmd + Space, type "VLC").
  2. Load the trailer playlist Go to File → Open File. Select the playlist file that contains the opening and closing trailers. If you have individual files instead of a playlist, drag them into the VLC window in the correct order: opening trailer first, closing trailer second.
  3. Confirm the order Open the playlist panel: View → Playlist. You should see two items — opening trailer on top, closing trailer below. Drag to reorder if needed.
  4. Pause immediately Press Space to start playback, then Space again to pause. This loads the first frame of the opening trailer so it is ready to go.
  5. Do not go fullscreen yet Leave VLC windowed for now. You will go fullscreen right before you play each trailer.

Set Up Google Slides

  1. Open the slide deck Open Chrome (or your browser) and navigate to the Google Slides presentation.
  2. Enter presenter mode Click the Slideshow button in the top right, or press Cmd + Shift + Enter to present from the first slide.
  3. Verify it fills the screen The slides should take over the full display. If you see browser chrome, press Cmd + Shift + F to toggle browser fullscreen.
  4. Exit presenter mode for now Press Esc to leave slideshow mode. You will re-enter it when the show starts.
Sound check Play a few seconds of the opening trailer with the room's speakers before anyone arrives. Adjust volume in VLC and on the room's audio system until it sounds right. Then pause the trailer and scrub back to the beginning (Cmd + Shift + Left Arrow to jump to start, or just press Home).

Running the Show

Here is the order of events and exactly what to do at each step.

Moment What to Do App
Opening trailer Switch to VLC (Cmd + Tab). Press F to go fullscreen. Press Space to play. VLC
Trailer ends Press F to exit fullscreen. Switch to Chrome (Cmd + Tab).
Slides begin Press Cmd + Shift + Enter to start slideshow from slide 1. Advance slides with Right Arrow or Space. Slides
Slides end Press Esc to exit slideshow mode. Slides
Closing trailer Switch to VLC (Cmd + Tab). The closing trailer should be next in the playlist. Press F for fullscreen. Press N to skip to the next track, or Space if it is already queued. Then Space to play. VLC
Closing trailer ends Press F to exit fullscreen. You are done. VLC
Keep it simple Between each transition, take a breath. The audience does not notice a two-second pause — they notice a wrong screen. Slow is smooth, smooth is fast.

If Something Goes Wrong

VLC plays the wrong trailer
Press Space to pause. Press N to skip to the next track, or open the playlist (View → Playlist) and double-click the correct file.
No sound from VLC
Check three things in order: (1) Is VLC muted? Press Cmd + Shift + Down to toggle. (2) Is Mac volume up? Use the physical volume keys. (3) Is the room audio system on and set to the right input?
Google Slides won't go fullscreen
Try Cmd + Shift + F to toggle browser fullscreen first, then click the Slideshow button. If presenter view opens on the wrong monitor, go to View → Presenter View and swap displays.
VLC is behind the browser and you can't find it
Press Cmd + Tab and look for the VLC cone icon. Click it to bring VLC forward.
The trailer ended and VLC jumped to the next file automatically
By default, VLC plays the next item in the playlist when a track finishes. If you want to prevent this, pause VLC as soon as the trailer ends. If VLC already started the closing trailer too early, press Space to pause immediately.
Everything froze
Press Esc to exit fullscreen or slideshow mode. If an app is unresponsive, press Cmd + Option + Esc to force-quit it, then reopen.
The golden rule If you are unsure what is happening, press Esc. It exits fullscreen, exits slideshow mode, and closes most dialogs. It always brings you back to a safe state.
